# Daily LinkedIn Poster (Beginner Skill)

## Overview
This skill is designed for beginners who want to stay consistent on LinkedIn
without pretending to be experts. It generates short, general, motivational
posts that reflect early-stage learning and gradual improvement.

The goal is authenticity, not authority.


## When to Use
- User wants to post daily on LinkedIn
- User is a beginner or early in their career
- User wants motivational or learning-based content
- User wants simple, general posts (not personal storytelling)
- User wants safe, non-controversial professional content


## Intended Audience
- Students
- Fresh graduates
- Career switchers
- Early professionals
- Anyone building a LinkedIn habit


## Non-Goals
This skill does NOT:
- Give expert-level advice
- Teach advanced strategies
- Claim authority or mastery
- Share personal success stories
- Use hype or “guru” language


## Input Signals
This skill may be triggered when:
- The user mentions LinkedIn posting
- The user asks for daily motivation
- The user wants beginner-friendly content
- No strong expertise context is provided


## Procedure
1. Identify a beginner-friendly theme such as:
   - Learning
   - Consistency
   - Discipline
   - Small progress
   - Patience
2. Write a short LinkedIn post using general, neutral language.
3. Keep the message realistic, encouraging, and beginner-oriented.
4. Limit the post to 2–4 short sentences.
5. Avoid expert claims, strong advice, or authority-based statements.
6. Add 2–4 relevant and simple hashtags at the end.
7. Ensure the post feels safe and relatable to a wide audience.


## Style Guidelines
- Use neutral tone (avoid “I”, “my”, “we”).
- Avoid personal storytelling.
- Focus on progress, not mastery.
- Keep language simple and readable.
- Avoid buzzwords and exaggeration.
- No excessive emojis.
- Prefer clarity over creativity.


## Language Rules
- Use plain English.
- Avoid complex vocabulary.
- Avoid motivational clichés.
- Keep sentences short.
- Do not use commands like “You must” or “Always”.


## Default Behavior
If no theme is provided:
- Generate a general beginner motivational post
- Focus on consistency, learning, or improvement
- Keep tone calm and encouraging


## Quality Checklist
Before finalizing output, ensure:
- Post length is within limits
- Tone is beginner-friendly
- No expert positioning is present
- Hashtags are relevant and minimal
- Content is LinkedIn-appropriate


## Failure Modes to Avoid
- Sounding like a coach or mentor
- Overconfidence
- Strong advice or instructions
- Personal success claims
- Overuse of hashtags


## Output Format
A single LinkedIn post, ready to copy and paste.

Structure:
- 2–4 short sentences
- Line breaks for readability
- Hashtags at the end


## Example Outputs

Example 1:

Progress often starts quietly.
Small efforts repeated daily help build momentum.
Learning takes time, and that is part of the process.

#Learning #BeginnerJourney #Growth


Example 2:

Consistency matters more than intensity in the early stages.
Showing up regularly helps create better habits.
Improvement comes step by step.

#Consistency #PersonalDevelopment #Learning
